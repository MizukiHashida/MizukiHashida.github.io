About Singapore Botanic Gardens


The Singapore Botanic Gardens is one of three gardens in the world honoured as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. When tourists travel to Singapore, the gardens would almost certainly be a part of their list of places to visit. From its variety of plant species to beautiful panoramic views of the natural landscape, many things attract visitors to this place. On top of that, you do not have to pay a single cent to enter the Singapore Botanic Gardens.



Highlights


You might wonder what makes The Singapore Botanic Gardens special and how it became a UNESCO World Heritage Site. I decided to answer your questions by traveling there and experiencing what the place has in store for its visitors.



Open Field


Enter through the gate on the northern side of the gardens and take the middle path when you arrive at the first crossroads. After a while you will find yourself in an open space with nothing but a few trees in sight. It is common for people to have a picnic in this area as it has a calming and relaxing environment. If you are bringing along your kids or pets, let them run around and have fun.

Ice Cream Man and Friends


Sometimes, the weather may not be in your favour. Located 137 kilometres above the equator, Singapore is no stranger to hot temperatures and rainy days. If you decide to take it down a notch and cool down, eating an ice cream is the way to go. You can find a restaurant nearby named Ice Cream Man and Friends where diners can enjoy delicious ice cream waffles with a variety of flavours to choose from.

How To Get There?


The Singapore Botanic Gardens is located at the heart of Singapore. Fortunately, traveling there is not as difficult as you may expect. It is due to the fact that there are not one but two train stations situated at the perimeter of the gardens. You may arrive on the northern side via the Botanic Gardens MRT Station or the southern side via the Napier MRT Station. However, be aware that you would need to walk a short distance from the Napier MRT Station to the south entrance of the gardens.
